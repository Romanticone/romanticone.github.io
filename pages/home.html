Xiang Liu is an Assistant Professor in Transportation Engineering at Rutgers University. He is also affliated with the Center for Advanced Infrastructure and Transportation (CAIT), one USDOT-designated national university transportation center. Xiang received his Ph.D. from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), with a focus on rail safety and risk analysis. His work develops an integrated risk management framework for maximizing rail safety (particularly hazardous materials transportation) in the most cost-efficient manner. Some of his research results have been used by the American railroad industry in development of several recent rail safety policies and practices.

Xiang's research aims to improve the safety, efficiency and resiliency of transportation systems, with a current focus on freight and logistics systems. He, his students and his colleagues endeavor to advance the state of the art in four inter-related areas: 1) Transportation Accident Analysis and Prevention; 2) Risk Analysis of Energy Transportation; 3) Optimization of Infrastructure Management; and 4) Transportation Statistics and Data Mining.
